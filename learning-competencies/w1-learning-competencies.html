Thinking About Time

May 7th 2015

Learning Competencies:

  • Research time management and productivity
    • Time management
    Time management is the process of organizing and planning how much time you spend on specific activities. Invest some time in our comprehensive collection of time management articles to learn about managing your own time more efficiently, and save yourself time in the future.
    Benefits of managing your time well can result to:
    • Greater productivity and efficiency
    • A better professional reputation.
    • Less Stress
    • Increased opportunities for advancement
    • Greater opportunities to achieve important life and career goals.
    Failing to manage your time can lead you to:
    • Missed deadlines
    • Inefficient workflow
    • Poor work quality
    • A poor professional repuration and a stalled career
    • Higher stress levels

    Productivity
    A measure of the efficiency of a person in converting inputs into outputs.
    Time management and productivity go hand in hand since the dedicated time you have managed for a certain task requires for you to be productive in doing the task in order to make the most out of the time set aside for the specific task.

  • Explain "Time Boxing" and explain how to use it

    • Time Box
    A previously agreed period of time during which a person or a team works steadily towards completion of some goal. Rather than to continuously work until the goal is reached and evaluating the time taken, the timebox approach consists of stopping work when the time limit is reached and evaluating what was accomplished.

    Time boxing depends on varying the time scales. A common technique that is used is the "pomdoro technique". This technique organizes personal work around 25-minute timeboxes with a 5 minute work break.

    Always stop work at the end of the time box and review your progress. Determine whether your goal has been met, or partially met if it included multiple tasks.

Your Command Line

May 7th 2015

Learning Competencies:

  • Explain what the "shell" is

    • A Shell is any user interface to the UNIX operating system. Any program that takes input from the user, translates it into instructions that the operating system can understand, and conveys the operating system's output back to the user.

  • Describe "bash"

    • A Bash is a Unix shell that acts as a software replacement for the Bourne shell. Bash is a command processor that typically runs in a text window where the user types commands that cause actions.
    There are two ways to use bash:
    1. As a user interface
    2. As a programming environment
By the end of this lesson, you should be able to ___ in/using the command line.
  • Identify your location

    • In order to identify my location, I would have to use the (pwd) command. This stands for Paths, Folders, and Directories

  • Navigate between directories

    • To navigate between directories, use the (cd) command. This stands for Change Directory.

  • Move, rename, and copy items

    • Rename: (mv) Move: (mv) Copy: (cp)

  • Create new and delete existing directories and files

    • Making an empty file: (touch) Make Directory: (mkdir) Delete Directory: (rmdir)

  • Open, view, or stream a file

    • View a file: (less, MORE) Stream a file: (cat)

  • Find and look inside files

    • Finding files: (find, DIR-R) Looking inside files: (find, DIR-R)

  • Get Help

    • Getting help: (apropos, HELP)
